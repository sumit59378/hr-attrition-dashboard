Interactive Analysis of Employee Attrition Patterns

Key Insights

  • Highest Risk: Males working 50+ hrs with WLB 4–5 hit 50% attrition — needs immediate attention
  • Gender Gap: Males in the <35 hr / low WLB bucket show 40% attrition vs 14.3% for females
  • WLB matters: WLB scores 8–10 consistently reduce attrition across all hour ranges
  • Sweet spot: 36–40 hrs/week + WLB 8–10 = 5–11% attrition range (the target zone)
  • Burnout signal: 50+ hour weeks show elevated attrition no matter the WLB score

Recommendations

  1. Workload review: Prioritise male employees working 50+ hours — mandatory check-ins
  2. WLB programs: Target employees scoring below 5 with flexible work options
  3. Hour caps: Set a soft ceiling on weekly hours, especially for high-attrition roles
  4. Dig deeper: Why are males in low-hour/low-WLB leaving 3x more than females?
  5. Scale what works: Replicate the 36–40 hr + high WLB conditions more broadly
